How SMS Opt-In Works in CedarPost

A Stetline LLC product · illustrations of the CedarPost mobile app's consent flow

CedarPost is members-only church software: text messaging reaches a person only after they've joined their own church in the app and turned text messages on themselves. The full flow, in order:

1

Install and join — with the church's approval

A new member installs CedarPost, creates an account, and enters their church's private invite code. That only files a request: a church administrator must approve them before they're a member at all. Nobody is in the system — let alone receiving messages — without both the invite code and staff approval.

2

Text messages are OFF by default

Every member controls their own notification channels from Notification Settings on their profile. The text-message toggle starts off — no member receives SMS unless they enable it themselves. There is no pre-checked box and no admin can turn it on for them.

3

The member opts in — with the terms in front of them

Enabling the toggle is the consent action, and the terms sit directly beneath it at the moment of choice:

By enabling, you agree to receive church announcement texts. Msg frequency varies. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP anytime to opt out.

Opt-in enabled, with the consent language visible under the toggle.

4

Opting out is always one step away

The member can turn text messages back off at any time on the same screen, or reply STOP to any message — either one ends SMS immediately. Replying HELP provides assistance. Mobile numbers and opt-in status are never shared with or sold to third parties for marketing — see our Privacy Policy.
