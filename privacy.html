Privacy Policy

Effective July 23, 2026 · CedarPost is a product of Stetline LLC

CedarPost is communication and directory software for churches. Your church runs its own private space in CedarPost: the church's staff decide who may join, and nothing in the app is visible to the public or to members of other churches. This policy explains what information we collect, how it's used, and the choices you have. We've written it to be read, not skimmed past.

What we collect

  • Account information — your email address and a password (stored in hashed form by our authentication provider), and the name you enter on your profile.
  • Church membership information — the profile details you choose to add (phone number, photo, birthday), your household, the ministries you belong to, and announcements you post. Churches may also keep volunteer safety records (such as background-check status) for members who serve with children; those records are entered and managed by your church's staff.
  • Notification preferences — your choices about how the app may contact you (push notifications, email, and text messages where offered).
  • Mobile phone numbers for text messaging — only if you opt in to receiving text messages from your church.

We don't collect anything else, and we don't run ads or analytics that profile you.

Your mobile number stays private. Mobile phone numbers and text-messaging opt-in information are never shared with or sold to third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Not by us, not by your church through CedarPost, not ever.

Text messaging (SMS)

  • Text messages are strictly opt-in. You will only receive them if you chose to.
  • Message frequency varies with your church's activity — a quiet week means few or no messages.
  • Message and data rates may apply, depending on your mobile carrier and plan.
  • Reply STOP at any time to stop receiving texts. Reply HELP for help. You can also change your notification settings in the app at any time.

Who can see your information

Only approved members of your own church — and even then, you control the details. The member directory is opt-in: you're not listed until you choose to be, and you choose which details (photo, phone, email, address, birthday) fellow members may see. Ministry leaders can see the roster of their own ministry. Children never appear in the directory; child profiles are created and managed by parents (or church staff), have no login of their own, and are visible only to their family, their ministry's leaders, and church staff.

We use a small number of service providers to run the app — database and file hosting, push notification delivery, email delivery, and text-message carriers. They process data only to deliver the service, never for their own marketing.

We do not sell personal information. We would disclose information only if the law genuinely required it.

How long we keep information

In plain terms, matching what church administrators see in our Admin Guide:

  • If you leave your church (or the church removes you): your directory listing, ministry memberships, notification settings, phone number, photo, and birthday are deleted. Your name remains on announcements you posted, and volunteer safety records are kept according to your church's policy.
  • If you delete your account: your sign-in is permanently destroyed and your name is removed from anything you posted (shown as "Removed Member" instead). This cannot be undone.
  • Volunteer safety records (for members who served with children) are the church's record of its own screening process and are retained even after a member leaves, per church policy and applicable law.

Your choices

Edit your profile and per-field directory visibility any time. Switch any notification channel off any time — your choices are always respected. Leave your church or delete your account from inside the app. For anything else, including questions about information your church holds about you, contact your church office or write to us directly.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy in a way that matters, we'll say so plainly on this page and update the date at the top.
